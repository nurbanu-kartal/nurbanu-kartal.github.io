Research

My research lies at the intersection of behavioural and experimental economics, microeconometrics, environmental economics, and labour economics. I use large-scale panel data, especially the German Socio-Economic Panel (SOEP), and experimental data to study time preferences, social comparisons, cognitive and non-cognitive skills, subjective well-being, well-being forecasts and environmental attitudes.

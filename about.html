ABOUT

Research shaped by behaviour, comparison, and lived economic experience.

PROFILE

I am a behavioural economist and microeconometrician working in the Economics department at the Turkish-German University, and I completed my PhD in Economics at Yildiz Technical University in 2026.

My research examines how social comparisons, time preferences, control beliefs, and cognitive and non-cognitive skills are associated with well-being, labour-market behaviour, and environmental attitudes.

I have held research visits at ZEW Mannheim, the University of Hohenheim, and the University of Gothenburg.

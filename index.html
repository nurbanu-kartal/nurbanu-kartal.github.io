BEHAVIOURAL AND EXPERIMENTAL ECONOMIST

I study how social comparisons, preferences, and skills shape economic behaviour and well-being.

I am a Research Assistant in Economics at the Turkish-German University and hold a PhD in Economics from Yildiz Technical University.

My work lies at the intersection of behavioural and experimental economics, microeconometrics, environmental economics, and labour economics.
